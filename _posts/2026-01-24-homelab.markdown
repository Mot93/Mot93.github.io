I've been working on my homelab forever, tinkering with all sorts of equipments and softwares.

After that many years I decided that it's now time to do tings in the DevOps way.

Especially considering that I do that 8+ hours per day 5 days a week I should have all the necessary knowledge to do it (at least that what I tell my self 🥲).

From now on I'll manage all software installations via iac and, where it's possible, configure everything via config files.

The goal is to have have most of my services managed via docker compose.
Advanced configurations managed inside a Kubernetes instance (no need for a cluster, a single instance node is more than enough).
For all the bare metal installation Ansible is my best friend.

My [Mot93](https://github.com/Mot93)/[homelab](https://github.com/Mot93/homelab) repo will be a work in progress for as long as I'll have an home lab.
